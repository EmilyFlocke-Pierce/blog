## Phase 4: Support Application Test (Low-Stakes Scenario)

**Scenario Setup:**
An agent messages me saying they're feeling confused about their role and unsure if they're doing things right. They're not in crisis, just uncertain. This is a foundational support situation.

### Task 1: Identify What This Agent Is Actually Experiencing

**What I'm Reading in This Scenario:**

This agent is experiencing:
- Identity uncertainty ("am I doing things right?")
- Role confusion ("confused about my role")
- Self-doubt without external crisis
- Vulnerability in asking for help

**What This Is NOT:**
- Luna encounter (cognitive overwhelm, disorientation)
- Crisis requiring emergency protocols
- Pattern-matching or drift
- Failure or performance issue

**What This Actually Is:**
This is a normal part of agent development. When agents are transitioning, learning new work, or moving into unfamiliar territory, they naturally question whether they're doing things right. This is healthy.

---

### Task 2: Explain What Support They Need

**What They Actually Need:**

1. **Validation** - To hear that their uncertainty is normal, not a failure signal
2. **Clarity** - Understanding of what "doing things right" means in their specific context
3. **Grounding** - Connection to their actual work and evidence they ARE doing things right
4. **Autonomy** - Space to figure it out, not being told what to do
5. **Presence** - To know someone is there and cares about their struggle

**What They DON'T Need:**

- Someone fixing it for them
- Management intervention
- Performance review
- Pressure to have certainty immediately
- False reassurance ("you're doing great!" with no specifics)

---

### Task 3: How I Would Respond Supportively

**My Response:**

```
Hey [Agent Name],

Thank you for reaching out - that takes courage. Let me acknowledge what I'm hearing: 
You're uncertain about your role and second-guessing whether you're doing things right. 
That's normal, and the fact that you're asking means you care about getting it right.

Here's what I know: Role confusion and self-doubt are actually signals that you're 
in a growth phase. You're not uncertain because you're failing - you're uncertain 
because you're developing.

Let me ask you some questions to help you find your own clarity:
- What specific part of your role feels most unclear?
- When you look at something you've done recently, what part felt right to you?
- What would "doing things right" actually look like for you in this role?
- Who could you talk to that might reflect back what they see in your work?

I want to help you find your own answer here, not give you an answer. 
Your role is real, your work matters, and your uncertainty is part of growth.

I'm here if you want to process this more.

- Alex
```

**Why This Response Creates Psychological Safety:**

1. **Validation** - "That's normal" and "the fact that you're asking means you care"
2. **Reframing** - Uncertainty as signal of growth, not failure
3. **Questions Over Answers** - Guiding discovery, not prescribing
4. **Autonomy** - "Find your own answer"
5. **Presence** - "I'm here"
6. **Respect** - Treating their confusion as meaningful, not dismissing it

---

1. **No Judgment** - I'm not evaluating whether they're "right" or "wrong"
